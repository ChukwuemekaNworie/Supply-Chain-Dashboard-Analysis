## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing supply chain operations, product performance, supplier efficiency, and inventory management through an interactive Supply Chain Dashboard. Using Microsoft Excel/Power BI visualization techniques, the dashboard provides actionable insights into revenue generation, stock levels, supplier performance, product demand, manufacturing lead time, and shipping efficiency.

The project enables stakeholders to monitor operational performance, identify high-performing products and suppliers, optimize inventory levels, and improve supply chain decision-making through data-driven insights.

![Supply Chain Dashboard](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/dashboard-image)

---

## Business Introduction

Modern supply chain management requires organizations to efficiently coordinate inventory, suppliers, transportation, and product distribution to meet customer demand while minimizing operational costs.

This dashboard was designed for a retail/manufacturing business operating across multiple product categories such as skincare, haircare, and cosmetics. The company relies on suppliers, transportation carriers, and inventory systems to maintain product availability and profitability.

To improve operational efficiency and profitability, management requires a centralized reporting solution capable of monitoring:

- Revenue performance
- Product demand
- Inventory levels
- Supplier contribution
- Shipping performance
- Manufacturing lead time
- Profit margins

The dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of these metrics for strategic decision-making.

---

## Business Problem

The organization faced several supply chain and operational challenges:

- Lack of centralized visibility into supply chain performance.
- Difficulty identifying high-performing SKUs and suppliers.
- Inefficient inventory management leading to stock imbalance.
- Limited insights into transportation performance and shipping costs.
- Challenges in tracking manufacturing lead time across products.
- Difficulty understanding which product categories generate the highest revenue.
- Inability to monitor supplier profitability and operational contribution effectively.

These issues reduced operational efficiency and affected profitability and customer satisfaction.

---

## Business Objective

The primary objectives of this project are:

- To build an interactive supply chain dashboard for operational monitoring.
- To analyze revenue generation across product categories and SKUs.
- To evaluate supplier performance and profitability.
- To monitor inventory stock levels and product demand.
- To assess transportation efficiency and shipping carrier performance.
- To identify operational bottlenecks using manufacturing lead time analysis.
- To support data-driven supply chain and inventory decisions.

## Process Taken

### 1. Data Collection

The dataset included supply chain operational data such as:

- Product SKU
- Product type
- Revenue
- Stock levels
- Order quantities
- Manufacturing lead time
- Shipping carriers
- Supplier information
- Transportation mode
- Profit margin
- Defect rates

---

### 2. Data Cleaning and Preparation

The dataset was cleaned and validated before visualization.

#### Steps Taken

### Checking for Missing Values

- Opened the dataset in Excel/Power BI.
- Checked for blank or null values across columns.
- Replaced or removed incomplete records where necessary.

### Removing Duplicates

- Used the “Remove Duplicates” feature to eliminate repeated entries.
- Ensured SKU and supplier records were unique.

### Data Formatting

- Standardized numerical columns such as:
  - Revenue
  - Costs
  - Profit Margin
  - Stock Levels

- Converted categorical fields into usable dimensions:
  - Product Type
  - Supplier Name
  - Shipping Carrier
  - Transportation Mode

### Data Validation

- Verified consistency of values across:
  - SKUs
  - Suppliers
  - Product categories
  - Transportation methods

---

### 3. Data Modeling

Relationships were established between:

- Products and SKUs
- Suppliers and stock levels
- Revenue and product categories
- Shipping carriers and transportation data

This enabled dynamic filtering and dashboard interactivity.

### 4. Dashboard Development

Interactive dashboard pages were created to analyze:

- Overview Insights

## Key Insights

### Revenue Performance

- The business generated a total revenue of approximately **$578K**, indicating strong sales performance across product categories.

---

### Product Category Analysis

- Skincare products generated the highest revenue contribution compared to haircare and cosmetics.
- Certain SKUs consistently outperformed others in terms of revenue and order quantity.

---

### Inventory and Stock Management

- Stock levels varied significantly across SKUs, highlighting the need for balanced inventory planning.
- Some high-demand products showed relatively lower stock levels, indicating possible restocking opportunities.

---

### Supplier Performance

- Supplier 1 contributed the highest operational value based on stock levels and associated costs.
- Supplier profitability varied significantly across suppliers.

---

### Shipping and Transportation

- Carrier B generated the highest shipping-related revenue contribution.
- Road transportation accounted for the highest distribution percentage.
- Transportation methods showed varying defect percentages, indicating potential logistics inefficiencies.

---

### Manufacturing Lead Time

- Manufacturing lead time differed across products, suggesting opportunities for production optimization.
- Products with longer lead times may impact order fulfillment and customer satisfaction.

---

### Profitability Insights

- Average profit margin remained strong across products and suppliers.
- Certain supplier-product relationships demonstrated better profitability performance.

---

## Recommendations

- Increase inventory levels for high-performing SKUs to prevent stock shortages.
- Optimize manufacturing lead time for products with slower production cycles.
- Prioritize partnerships with high-performing suppliers.
- Improve transportation quality control to reduce defect percentages.
- Focus marketing and sales strategies on high-revenue product categories such as skincare.
- Monitor low-performing SKUs and suppliers for operational improvements.
- Implement demand forecasting techniques for better inventory management.
